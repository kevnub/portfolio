Case Study

Introduction

Enova is a biomimicry engineering group on campus at the University of Calgary that contacted me to redesign their website. They had already designed and implemented code for their club website but wanted something that looked and felt more professional.
This project took less than a week to complete since I was able to frequently meet up with the client to make sure I was providing everything they wanted.

Since I was the one to implement the code for the website, I designed something that was within my coding capabilities. I wanted to take advantage of the MaterializeCSS framework that I used to create this website and, as a result, I was able to quickly come up with high-fidelity prototypes for the client.

The Home Page

The original design for the homepage was not very user friendly. Each of the letters in the word "Innovate" were links that led to different parts of their website (see below), but there was no indication of where each link would take the user, so I decided to scrap the idea altogether. There is also the issue where the words "Innovate", "Educate", and "Promote" were too large to even fit on a screen without having to zoom out.

From asking the club members some general questions, it seemed that the visitors of this website would be potential sponsors and people in the engineering industry who wanted to know more about the club. I then designed the homepage to clearly show the club's name, tagline, and mission statement.

The before and after:

You can also see that I removed the "Events" tab, and instead placed the events information on the home page. I did this because the events were a more important aspect for the club's success and I wanted it to be displayed with the same importance as the club's mission statement.

The home page is also where the navigation bar is introduced; this nav-bar will be the header of the rest of the pages. (And a similar footer was also added). You'll notice that I did not include the "About" section in the redesigned website because it was very similar to the Mission Statements seen on the homepage and wasn't necessary or important enough to have its own page.

Lastly, I updated the color scheme to reflect a sleeker, more sophisticated feel. I kept the same general color scheme of green, white, and grey; but selected different shades and made sure there was a balance of the three colors in the final design.

Photo Gallery

I didn't think that there was too much to be improved with the photo gallery. The main problem with the original photo gallery was that none of the images were compressed, which resulted in a very long page-loading time.

The before and after:

The original and redesigned photo gallery both have the funcionality of images enlarging when they are clicked. I debated whether to put the photos in collapsible tabs and figured that both ways would work depending on the amount of photos to be included, which was undecided as of now.

Archive

For the Archive page, I reduced the cluttered look by introducing collapsible elements where each title will expand to reveal its description and extra information. I also made the body of the page more centered for a more attractive visual design.

Other than that, there wasn't much to change except to make the general layout more consistent with the redesigned look.

The before and after:

Contact

I included a form that allowed users to send messages to the club directly from the website for more convenience. I'm actually on the fence about this idea because I feel like some users would prefer to send emails from their own peronsal or business email in order to have the actual email in their sent box or simply to be able to keep track of the message.

The risk of the "send message" form is that it may not be utilized by the user.
